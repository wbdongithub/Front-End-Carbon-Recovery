# Pyrolysis and Contaminant Management

![Containerized continuous pyrolysis equipment](/Front-End-Carbon-Recovery/img/pyrolysis-unit.png)

Pyrolysis heats dry organic solids with little or no oxygen. The feed is converted into a carbon-rich solid, gases, and condensable products. In the FECR concept, the gases are sent to a high-temperature combustion system rather than released untreated.

## What this stage is intended to do

- Greatly reduce the remaining solids volume
- Convert organic material into stable carbonaceous char
- Destroy pathogens and many organic contaminants under validated conditions
- Recover useful heat from the process gases

## PyroNexus

The vendor formerly known as ARTi is now **PyroNexus**. Its continuous, modular equipment is one example of the kind of pyrolysis system that may fit FECR. Vendor-specific capacities and claims should be verified for the actual biosolids feed through testing, permitting review, and a full mass and energy balance.

## Important boundary

This FECR site follows the material through production of biochar. Detailed discussion of biochar activation, PFAS adsorption, nZVI modification, and wastewater-cleanup applications belongs in a separate Biochar Knowledge Base.
