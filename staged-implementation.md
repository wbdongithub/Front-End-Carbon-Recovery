# The FECR Process

![Front End Carbon Recovery flow diagram](/Front-End-Carbon-Recovery/img/fecr-flow.png)

The process begins just downstream of the grit chamber, where raw wastewater passes through a **microscreen**, also called a **rotating belt filter (RBF)** in scientific literature.

## Core stages

1. **Microscreening** captures a large fraction of incoming suspended solids, especially fibrous cellulose from toilet paper and laundry lint.
2. **Mechanical dewatering** in an integral screw press increases solids concentration to roughly 35–40% in favorable applications.
3. **Low-temperature drying** can produce a stable, transportable Class A material.
4. **Pyrolysis** converts the dry material into biochar while volatile products are treated in a high-temperature combustion system.

Each stage can stand on its own. A utility may initially install only the microscreen, then add drying or pyrolysis later.
