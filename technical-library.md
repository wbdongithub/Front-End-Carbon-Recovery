# Low-Temperature Heat-Pump Drying

![Low-temperature belt dryer process diagram](/Front-End-Carbon-Recovery/img/shincci-process.png)

Low-temperature belt dryers circulate warm, dehumidified air through a closed chamber. Moisture evaporated from the solids is condensed, and the heat-pump cycle recovers much of the latent heat.

## Role in FECR

Drying can be a later implementation stage rather than part of the first project. It provides several independent benefits:

- Stable, transportable material
- Lower hauling weight
- Potential production of Class A biosolids under an approved operating regime
- A controlled feedstock for centralized pyrolysis

Because microscreen solids can enter the dryer at a higher solids concentration than ordinary dewatered sludge, the dryer can be smaller and consume less energy for the same dry-solids throughput.

## Equipment neutrality

Shincci equipment is a useful commercial example and source of process illustrations, but FECR is not tied to one manufacturer. The design requirement is efficient, controlled drying compatible with the recovered feedstock and final regulatory objective.
