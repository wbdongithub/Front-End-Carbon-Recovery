Implementation Strategy: FECR in Stages

FECR should not be presented as an all-or-nothing capital project. Each stage provides value on its own while preserving a path to the full process.

StagePrimary goalWhy it matters
1 Microscreen retrofitRelieve solids loadingImmediate operational value for plants showing solids stress.
2 Solids managementHandle dewatered front-end solidsMay allow landfill, beneficial reuse, or integration with existing programs.
3 Heat-pump dryingProduce stable Class A materialReduces hauling and creates transportable feedstock.
4 PyrolysisDestroy contaminants and reduce volumeProduces biochar and can support regional solids management.
5 Biochar usesCreate value from carbonHandled mainly in the companion Biochar Knowledge Base.

Why this matters for customers

A utility may not have funding, confidence, or regulatory support for the complete train at the start. A staged roadmap lets them solve the immediate problem first and defer later steps until the evidence and economics are stronger.

Before design, most utilities will want proof at their own plant. That leads naturally to the demonstration program.
