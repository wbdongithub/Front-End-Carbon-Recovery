# Why Front End Carbon Recovery?

Front End Carbon Recovery (FECR) is a practical strategy for reducing the solids burden on an existing wastewater treatment plant by removing a substantial portion of incoming suspended solids near the headworks.

The immediate goal is not to redesign the entire plant. It is to relieve the processes that are already working hardest.

## The basic idea

Conventional treatment allows most incoming solids to pass through several downstream processes before they are finally removed as sludge. During that journey, the solids may:

- consume aeration energy;
- contribute to biological sludge production;
- increase clarifier and digester loading;
- require polymer, thickening and dewatering;
- increase hauling and disposal costs.

FECR intercepts much of the fibrous, carbon-rich material before it becomes back-end sludge.

> **Recover solids early. Everything downstream has less work to do.**

## A retrofit first

The strongest near-term application is an existing plant experiencing increasing solids-related stress. FECR can also be incorporated into a new plant, but that is not the primary emphasis of this first website version.

## Modular by design

A utility can install only the microscreen and dewatering stage, then add drying or pyrolysis later. Each stage has a useful purpose on its own.
