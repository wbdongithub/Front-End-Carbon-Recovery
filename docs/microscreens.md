# Microscreens (Rotating Belt Filters)

The terms **microscreen** and **Rotating Belt Filter (RBF)** refer to the same general technology. This site uses *microscreen* as the preferred plain-language term and uses *RBF* when discussing scientific literature.

## How it works

Wastewater passes through a moving fine-mesh belt. Fibrous solids collect on the belt and are carried upward. The solids are removed near the top roller and transferred to an integral dewatering screw press.

![See-through microscreen model](/img/microscreen-model-a.png)

The transparent model makes the principle visible: long cellulose fibers are captured while much smaller particles pass through. Belt speed can also be adjusted to permit a filter mat to develop and capture finer solids.

## Why cellulose matters

A substantial fraction of municipal influent solids is cellulose, largely from toilet paper and laundry lint. This material is fibrous and mechanically dewaters more readily than biological sludge.

## Where it can be installed

The most direct FECR arrangement places the microscreen on raw wastewater downstream of grit removal. A portable demonstration unit can also explore other locations, including concentrated primary solids streams.
