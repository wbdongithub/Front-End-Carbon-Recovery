# Staged Implementation

FECR is designed so that a utility can solve today's problem without committing immediately to the entire treatment train.

<div className="stageGrid">
<div className="stageCard">
### Stage 1 — Demonstrate
Use a portable unit to measure capture, cake dryness and effects at candidate installation points.
</div>
<div className="stageCard">
### Stage 2 — Install the microscreen
Relieve downstream solids loading and create a mechanically dewatered solids stream.
</div>
<div className="stageCard">
### Stage 3 — Improve solids management
Landfill, transport or otherwise manage the dewatered material under applicable local requirements.
</div>
<div className="stageCard">
### Stage 4 — Add drying
Produce Class A biosolids, low-moisture material and prepare for economical transport or future pyrolysis.
</div>
<div className="stageCard">
### Stage 5 — Add pyrolysis
Destroy organic contaminants, reduce mass and produce biochar.
</div>
<div className="stageCard">
### Stage 6 — Develop biochar outlets
Address carbon storage or product uses in a separate, more specialized program, potentially for wastewater cleanup.
</div>
</div>

## Why staging matters

Staging reduces financial and technical risk. The utility gains real operating data at each step, and earlier equipment remains useful if later stages are delayed.
