# Is My Plant a Candidate for FECR?

FECR may warrant evaluation when several of the following conditions are present:

- The plant is approaching a planned expansion.
- Hydraulic and solids-related constraints appear to be developing together.
- Clarifiers, digesters or dewatering systems have little reserve capacity.
- Sludge hauling or disposal costs are increasing.
- Water conservation has increased influent concentration.
- Plant staff observe solids-overload symptoms even though the original design basis is hard to locate.
- Additional digesters or solids facilities would be very expensive.
- The utility wants a staged project rather than one large capital commitment.
- Biosolids regulations or contaminant concerns are making current outlets less secure.

This checklist does not replace engineering analysis. It identifies situations where a demonstration and preliminary mass balance may be worthwhile.
