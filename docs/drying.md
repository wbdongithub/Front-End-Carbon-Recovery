# Low-Temperature Heat-Pump Drying

Drying is an optional FECR stage. It can stabilize the solids, reduce transport weight and prepare a consistent feedstock for pyrolysis.

## Why heat-pump drying fits FECR

A closed, low-temperature system circulates heated air through the solids and recovers heat as water vapor is condensed. The principal energy input is electricity for compressors, fans and material handling.

## Independent value

A utility may stop at this stage and produce a much drier, more transportable material. Drying therefore does not depend on immediate construction of a pyrolysis facility.

## Design caution

Claims concerning Class A biosolids, pathogen reduction, energy consumption and final solids concentration must be tied to the selected equipment, operating conditions and regulatory approval. Vendor literature is useful, but project-specific confirmation is necessary.
