# Microscreens / Rotating Belt Filters

A **microscreen** is also known in the scientific literature as a **Rotating Belt Filter (RBF)**. The terms are used interchangeably on this site.

For FECR, the microscreen is the enabling technology. It removes suspended solids and fibrous cellulosic material from raw wastewater near the headworks.

## How it works

Wastewater flows through a moving fine-mesh belt. Fibers and suspended solids collect on the belt. The belt carries the solids out of the wastewater stream, where they are removed and sent to a dewatering device.

The filter can operate in different modes. A faster belt tends to capture larger fibrous solids. A slower belt allows a mat to build on the screen, which can capture smaller particles but reduces hydraulic capacity.

## Why cellulose matters

A significant fraction of incoming suspended solids is cellulosic material from toilet paper and washing-machine lint. These fibers are relatively easy to capture and dewater compared with biological sludge.

## Terminology

| Preferred FECR term | Other terms in literature |
|---|---|
| Microscreen | Rotating Belt Filter, RBF, Rotating Belt Sieve, RBS |
| Cellulosic solids | Primary cellulosic sludge, cellulosic screenings, filter primary sludge |
