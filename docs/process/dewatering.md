# Dewatering

The first major economic advantage of FECR comes from removing water mechanically before relying on evaporation.

Microscreen solids are usually rich in fibers. Much of the water is held between fibers rather than inside biological floc. This makes the material easier to squeeze than waste activated sludge or digested sludge.

## Why early solids are different

Back-end sludge is normally difficult to dewater and often leaves the dewatering process at roughly 20 percent solids.

By contrast, fibrous solids recovered at the front of the plant can often be pressed to much higher solids content. In the FECR concept, the target is typically around 35–40 percent solids before drying.

## Why this matters

Drying is mostly a water removal cost. Every gallon of water removed mechanically is a gallon that does not need to be evaporated later.

This makes the drying stage smaller, cheaper, and easier to justify.
