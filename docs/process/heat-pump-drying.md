# Heat-Pump Drying

Once solids have been mechanically dewatered, thermal drying becomes a practical next stage.

Low-temperature heat-pump dryers remove water in a closed or controlled system by circulating warm air, condensing moisture, and recycling heat. This allows dried biosolids to be produced using electricity rather than steam or direct combustion.

## Role in FECR

Drying can be a standalone stage or a preparation step for pyrolysis.

A utility may stop here if the immediate goal is to produce a stable, transportable, Class A biosolids product. Pyrolysis can be added later.

## Why it fits after microscreening

The dryer required after FECR can be much smaller than a dryer sized for wet back-end sludge because the microscreen and screw press have already removed much of the water.
