# Pyrolysis

Pyrolysis heats dried organic solids in a low-oxygen environment. The process converts the solids into biochar, gases, vapors, and recoverable heat.

In FECR, pyrolysis is the stage that converts a biosolids management problem into a carbon-rich product while providing a pathway for destruction of organic contaminants in the solids and off-gas.

## Vendor-neutral framing

This site describes the pyrolysis stage generically. Commercial systems may differ in reactor design, temperature, residence time, emissions control, heat recovery, feed handling, and biochar production rate.

The vendor previously referenced as ARTi is now referred to as **PyroNexus** in project materials.

## Keep the scope disciplined

Pyrolysis produces biochar, but biochar applications are a separate large topic. FECR should explain that biochar can be useful without trying to become a full biochar science site.
