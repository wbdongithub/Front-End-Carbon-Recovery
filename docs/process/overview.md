# The FECR Process

Front End Carbon Recovery is best understood as a staged treatment train.

<div className="processLine">Raw wastewater
  ↓
Headworks / grit removal
  ↓
Microscreen (Rotating Belt Filter)
  ↓
Integral screw press
  ↓
Dewatered carbon-rich solids
  ↓
Optional heat-pump drying
  ↓
Optional pyrolysis
  ↓
Biochar and recovered energy</div>

The first stage, microscreening, can provide value by itself. Drying and pyrolysis can be added later as funding, regulations, and project goals evolve.

## Why the process starts early

Solids removed at the beginning of the plant do not have to pass through biological treatment, secondary clarification, digestion, dewatering, and disposal.

That early removal is what makes the process valuable.

## Why the process is modular

Not every utility is ready for a complete drying and pyrolysis system. FECR allows a plant to begin with the stage that solves its immediate problem and add later stages when they make sense.
