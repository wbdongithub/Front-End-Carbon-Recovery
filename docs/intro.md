# Front End Carbon Recovery

**Front End Carbon Recovery (FECR)** is a practical, modular strategy for improving wastewater treatment plant performance by recovering carbon-rich solids near the beginning of the plant.

The initial focus is not to replace the wastewater treatment plant. The initial focus is to **unload existing plants**, especially facilities where solids handling, sludge production, digesters, aeration, or biosolids management are becoming operational constraints.

FECR can also be incorporated into new plant designs, but the first and most important use case is as a retrofit for existing plants.

## Core idea

> Recover solids early. Everything downstream becomes easier.

FECR typically begins just downstream of the grit chamber with a **microscreen**, also known in the technical literature as a **Rotating Belt Filter (RBF)**.

The recovered solids are rich in cellulose and primary organic matter. They are much easier to dewater than conventional biological sludge, and they can become feedstock for drying, pyrolysis, and biochar production.
