# Is My Plant a Candidate for FECR?

FECR deserves consideration when several of the following conditions are present:

- The plant is approaching a planned expansion or major rehabilitation.
- Solids-handling equipment is a recurring bottleneck.
- Digesters, clarifiers, or dewatering systems are difficult to take out of service.
- Biosolids hauling and disposal costs are increasing.
- The community has conserved water while population and solids loading have grown.
- Operators see signs of solids stress even though the exact design limit is uncertain.
- The utility wants a staged project rather than one large, all-or-nothing investment.
- Future restrictions on land application or contaminant management are a concern.

## The next practical step

A candidate plant should not begin with a full commercial installation. A focused desktop review and a field demonstration can establish:

1. Where a microscreen could be installed.
2. How much TSS and cellulose can be removed.
3. The achievable dewatered solids concentration.
4. Effects on downstream carbon and nutrient balances.
5. The most useful first implementation stage.
