# Why FECR Is Different

Traditional wastewater treatment usually allows most incoming carbon-rich solids to pass into the treatment plant, where they are settled, biologically processed, digested, dewatered, hauled, or disposed of.

FECR changes the question.

Instead of asking how to manage more sludge at the back end of the plant, FECR asks:

> Can a significant portion of the solids be recovered before they become a downstream burden?

## Traditional thinking

Build more biological treatment, more digesters, more dewatering equipment, or more solids handling capacity.

## FECR thinking

Remove a substantial portion of the suspended solids before they stress the rest of the plant.

## Why this matters

Early solids are different from back-end sludge.

Front-end recovered solids are rich in fibrous and cellulosic material. Water is held between fibers and can often be removed mechanically. Back-end biological sludge is normally much wetter and more difficult to dewater.

This is why FECR is not simply another solids disposal option. It is a way to avoid creating some of the difficult sludge that would otherwise appear later.
