# Is My Plant a Candidate for FECR?

A plant does not need to have a formally documented solids limit to be a candidate for FECR. In many cases, the practical question is whether solids loading is creating operational stress.

## Candidate indicators

FECR may deserve evaluation if several of these are true:

- The plant is approaching its design capacity.
- Operators see symptoms of solids overload.
- Digesters are a bottleneck.
- Dewatering equipment is struggling.
- Sludge is wet and expensive to haul.
- Aeration energy is increasing.
- A plant expansion is being discussed.
- Biosolids disposal options are becoming more restricted.
- The utility wants a staged approach rather than a single major capital project.

## First evaluation step

The first step is usually not a full-scale installation. It is a practical evaluation of where a microscreen could be placed and what solids it would remove.

A portable demonstration unit can be especially useful because it allows multiple locations in the plant to be tested under real conditions.
