# Implement FECR in Stages

A utility does not need to fund or approve the entire treatment train at once.

## Stage 1 — Microscreen retrofit

Install one or more microscreens to relieve an existing plant. The immediate goal is reduced downstream loading and a measurable reduction in the solids that eventually become back-end sludge.

## Stage 2 — Practical solids management

Use the pressed material in the best available permitted outlet. This may be landfill disposal, an existing solids program, or transport to a regional processor.

## Stage 3 — Add low-temperature drying

Produce a stable, lower-weight material that is easier to store and transport. Drying can also prepare the feed for centralized pyrolysis.

## Stage 4 — Add pyrolysis

Convert the dried material into biochar, destroy organic contaminants under validated conditions, and recover heat.

## Why phasing matters

Phasing reduces technical and financial risk. The utility can verify actual capture, dewatering, operating cost, and downstream benefits before committing to later stages. Earlier investments remain useful rather than becoming stranded assets.
