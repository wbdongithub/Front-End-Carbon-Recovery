# Microscreens, Also Called Rotating Belt Filters

A microscreen is a continuous belt filtration device used to remove suspended solids from wastewater. In research papers it is often called a **rotating belt filter (RBF)** or **rotating belt sieve (RBS)**. This site uses *microscreen* as the preferred plain-language term and uses *RBF* when referring to literature.

Raw sewage enters the deep end of the unit. Water passes through the moving screen while fibers collect on the belt. As the belt rises, the captured material is removed near the upper roller and transferred to a screw press.

## Why cellulose is important

A substantial fraction of incoming municipal solids is cellulosic, mainly from toilet paper and laundry lint. The fibers are long compared with bacteria and are therefore well suited to physical capture.

## Operating control

Belt speed can influence performance. A faster belt tends to emphasize fiber capture. A slower belt allows a filter mat to develop, which can capture smaller particles but reduces hydraulic capacity.

## Retrofit locations

The preferred location is often on raw wastewater after grit removal, but a demonstration unit can evaluate other locations such as primary solids streams or flows directed toward digestion.
