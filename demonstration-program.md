# Dewatering the Recovered Solids

The solids recovered by a microscreen are different from conventional waste activated sludge. They contain a large fibrous fraction, and much of the water is held between fibers rather than inside a biological floc.

That physical difference is why front-end solids can often be pressed to approximately **35–40% total solids**, while conventional dewatered back-end sludge is often closer to **20% total solids**.

## Why this matters

Drying cost is largely a water-removal cost. Starting at 35–40% solids instead of 20% means substantially less water must be evaporated for every dry ton of material.

The integral screw press is therefore not a minor accessory. It is one of the key reasons the FECR process can become practical.

## Interim value

At some sites, pressed solids may be suitable for landfill disposal after applicable testing and regulatory review. This allows a utility to gain the front-end loading benefits before committing to a dryer or pyrolysis system.
